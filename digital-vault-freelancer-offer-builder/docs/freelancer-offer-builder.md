# Freelancer Offer Builder

## Overview
Freelancer Offer Builder is a static Digital Vault mini-tool for creating professional offer drafts with structured scope, package communication, delivery assumptions, and practical next steps.

## Purpose
Help freelancers and studios move from scattered notes to clear, client-ready offers without turning the tool into a price calculator.

## How to use
1. Fill offer context details.
2. Select the most relevant package.
3. Choose core deliverables and optional add-ons.
4. Define milestones, timeline assumptions, and revisions.
5. Add payment/collaboration terms, responsibilities, and exclusions.
6. Choose tone and add optional investment note.
7. Review generated offer, email intro, and internal notes.
8. Copy, print, save, or reset.

## Offer context guide
Provide client/project identifiers, offer title, project type, industry, validity date, prepared-by details, contact, manual investment note, and internal notes.

## Packages
- **Starter**: small launches, one-page sites, validation offers.
- **Business**: multi-page business websites with core trust/conversion goals.
- **Premium**: stronger UX/presentation polish and broader quality requirements.
- **Custom**: advanced integrations, non-standard delivery, or custom functionality.

## Core deliverable categories
- Strategy & Planning
- Design / UI
- Development
- SEO / Performance
- Deployment
- Handoff

## Optional add-ons
Includes scoped extras such as content pages, copy support, integrations, CMS/WordPress, e-commerce module, maintenance, analytics review, and speed improvements.

## Timeline and milestones guidance
Use duration, revision rounds, and launch window to set delivery assumptions. Select milestones to communicate phase-based execution clearly.

## Payment and collaboration terms guidance
Use practical operational terms (deposit, milestones, final payment, feedback limits, legal-content responsibility, and third-party cost boundaries). This is not legal advice.

## Client responsibilities
Clarifies client inputs required for smooth delivery: copy, assets, approvals, access, legal content, decision maker, and payment compliance.

## Exclusions
Explicitly define what is outside scope (e.g., legal writing, paid subscriptions, custom backend, unlimited revisions, advanced campaigns).

## Complexity scoring
The tool uses weighted scope items to classify the offer:
- 0–20: Simple Offer
- 21–45: Standard Offer
- 46–75: Advanced Offer
- 76+: Custom / High-Complexity Offer

This is for scope planning and communication only, not automatic pricing.

## Risk / attention notes
Automatic checks flag potential risks:
- scope expansion
- content readiness
- technical dependencies
- revision ambiguity
- payment safeguards
- legal/content ownership dependencies
- maintenance gaps after technical delivery

## Sample offer structure
1. Offer Summary
2. Recommended Package
3. Project Scope
4. Core Deliverables
5. Optional Add-ons
6. Timeline & Milestones
7. Client Responsibilities
8. Payment & Collaboration Terms
9. What Is Not Included
10. Investment Note
11. Recommended Next Steps

## Sample client email intro
Hi [Client Name],

Thank you for sharing the project details for [Project Name].

Based on the current scope, I prepared the offer below. It includes the recommended package, core deliverables, optional add-ons, timeline assumptions, and next steps.

Please review the scope and let me know if anything should be clarified before we move forward.

Best,

## Practical recommendations
- Keep package selection aligned with true scope depth.
- Use optional add-ons to prevent hidden scope creep.
- Confirm content ownership and legal responsibility early.
- Lock revision rounds before design/development begins.
- Require deposit before production for risk control.
- Phase complex additions when timelines are tight.
