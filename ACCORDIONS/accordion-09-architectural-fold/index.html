The threshold composition balances honed stone, recessed oak, and narrow metallic seams so the arrival feels intentional rather than ornamental. The unfolded panel language mirrors the way a wall plane reveals hidden depth only when approached from the correct angle.

Spatially, the sequence is calibrated for hospitality-grade calm: muted reflection, measured contrast, and circulation cues that read as part of the architecture instead of applied décor.

This study explores how crisp geometry can quietly shape acoustics, intimacy, and movement. A sequence of offset planes forms a controlled edge that screens seating while preserving long sightlines across the room.

The open content surface is conceived as the inner face of that partition: warmer in tone, slightly more tactile, and reserved for the most detailed narrative information.

When translated into interface form, that logic becomes an accordion that feels built into the surface around it—stable, tactile, and editorially precise.
