3) Discovery Question Bank

Business goals

  • What outcomes should this project produce in the next 6-12 months?
  • Which business metrics matter most (leads, bookings, sales, applications)?
  • Why is this project a priority right now?

Target audience

  • Who is the ideal visitor or customer you want to attract?
  • What objections or questions do prospects usually have?
  • What action should visitors take on the new site?

Current website problems

  • What is not working well on the current site?
  • Where do users drop off or fail to convert?
  • What feedback have you received from customers or team members?

Project scope

  • Which pages, sections, or features are required for launch?
  • Do you need integrations (CRM, email tools, booking, payments)?
  • What must be included in phase one vs later phases?

Content and assets

  • Who is responsible for copy, images, and brand assets?
  • What content already exists, and what still needs to be created?
  • Will your team need guidance on content structure or messaging?

Budget and timeline

  • What investment range has been approved for this project?
  • Is the deadline fixed or flexible?
  • Are there events or launches driving the timeline?

Decision process

  • Who signs off on budget, scope, and final approval?
  • How many stakeholders will review deliverables?
  • What does your vendor selection process look like?

5) Red Flags to Watch During the Call

  • No clear business objective for the project
  • No confirmed budget or unwillingness to discuss investment
  • Unrealistic deadline with unclear constraints
  • Too many decision makers with no final owner
  • Unclear responsibility for content and approvals
  • Missing core assets needed to start delivery
  • Scope changes repeatedly before requirements are agreed
